What it does

Set it up once and forget it. From 9 PM to 10 AM the TV simply won't stay on — the instant someone powers it up, it drops straight back to standby. Outside those hours it does absolutely nothing and never gets in your way. Perfect as a gentle, no-arguments bedtime rule for a shared TV.

An honest heads-up: Android gives apps no way to truly cut a device's power — that's locked to the manufacturer and the system. So "turn it off" here means put the TV into standby (screen off), exactly what the remote's power button does. In everyday use it feels identical: the screen goes dark and stays dark. (On a rooted TV it can do a real power-off instead — one line of code.)

How it works

A quiet background service watches for the TV waking up. If the clock is inside curfew, it locks the screen straight back off. Four little safety nets make sure it always wins:

⚡ Instant

The moment the TV wakes during curfew, it's locked again before you see anything.

🕐 Heartbeat

A timer re-checks every 60 seconds through the whole window, as a backstop.

🔄 Survives reboots

Unplug and replug the TV? It quietly starts itself back up.

🛡️ Hard to kill

Even if the TV's power-saver kills the app, a scheduled watchdog brings it right back.

Get it on your TV

1
Download the app. Grab tv-curfew.apk from the latest release and sideload it onto your Android TV.
2
Turn on the permission. Open the TV Curfew app and tap its two buttons — “Enable Device Admin” then “Start service”. That's the whole setup.
3
Done. It now runs invisibly in the background forever. There's nothing to open again.
Prefer the command line? (ADB, no clicking)  ›

Enable Developer options → Network debugging on the TV, then from a computer on the same network:

adb connect <TV-IP>:5555
adb install -r tv-curfew.apk
# grant the lock permission — works from adb shell, no root needed:
adb shell dpm set-active-admin com.tatav.tvcurfew/.CurfewAdminReceiver
adb shell am start-foreground-service com.tatav.tvcurfew/.CurfewService

Remove later: adb shell dpm remove-active-admin com.tatav.tvcurfew/.CurfewAdminReceiver then adb uninstall com.tatav.tvcurfew

Under the hood

For the curious (or the developer forking this): here's the build, the code map, and the proof it works.

The pieces

FileRole
CurfewLogic.ktThe time-gate: hour >= 21 || hour < 10. Unit-tested.
CurfewEnforcer.ktDecides & performs the lock (lockNow()).
CurfewService.ktForeground service holding the screen-on watcher.
CurfewAlarmReceiver.kt60 s heartbeat + next-9 PM alarm.

MainActivity.ktThe tiny two-button setup screen.

Change the hours in one place — START_HOUR / END_HOUR in CurfewLogic.kt.

Proof it works

Tested live on an Android TV emulator with the clock set inside the curfew window:

# app starts -> immediately locks to standby
enforce(service_start): IN curfew -> lockNow()   →   mWakefulness=Asleep

# someone "turns the TV on" with the remote...
screen event: SCREEN_ON  →  enforce(screen_on): IN curfew -> lockNow()
                                              →   back to Asleep in ~4s
That's the whole feature: turn it on during curfew → it goes right back to sleep, no prompt. And a matching set of 9 unit tests confirms 9 PM–10 AM locks while noon / 8 PM stay allowed.

Good to know

  • It's standby, not a full power-off. The screen goes dark and stays dark — like the power button. A true shutdown isn't possible for a normal app (needs root).
  • It follows the TV's clock. Change the TV's time and the window moves with it.
  • Some TV brands aggressively close background apps. The built-in watchdog handles that (worst case it re-locks within ~15 min; instantly otherwise). If your brand is extreme, also allow the app to run in the background in settings.
