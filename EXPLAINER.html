☾ TV Curfew

An Android TV app that forces the TV into standby whenever anyone turns it on between 9:00 PM and 10:00 AM — no warning, no prompt. Built & verified from the command line.
KotlinminSdk 21 · target 34 AGP 8.9 · Gradle 8.13DeviceAdmin lockNow() No rootTested on Android TV emulator (API 34)

1 · The one hard truth first

Android has no public API to fully power a device off. PowerManager has no shutdown method; the real SHUTDOWN/REBOOT permissions are signature|privileged — granted only to platform-signed system apps or root. A sideloaded personal app cannot cut the power unless the TV is rooted.

So "shut it down" is implemented as the next best thing that needs no root and behaves identically to pressing the remote's power button:

DeviceAdmin → DevicePolicyManager.lockNow() → standby (screen off). Paired with a watcher that re-locks instantly on every wake, the result is exactly the goal: the moment anyone turns the TV on during curfew, it drops straight back to standby.

2 · How the enforcement works

Three independent mechanisms cover every way the TV can come alive during the window:

⚡ Instant re-lock

A foreground service holds a runtime ACTION_SCREEN_ON receiver (that broadcast can't be declared in the manifest since Android 7). Any wake → check the clock → if in curfew, lockNow(). Reaction time ≈ instant.

🕑 60-second heartbeat

While inside curfew, an exact AlarmManager alarm re-arms itself every 60 s and re-locks — a backstop in case a screen-on broadcast is ever missed. Outside curfew it sleeps until the next 9 PM.

🔄 Survives reboot

A BOOT_COMPLETED / MY_PACKAGE_REPLACED receiver restarts the service after a power cycle or app update, so turning the TV on during curfew locks it before you see anything.

🎵 Single source of truth

Every path calls one gate, CurfewLogic.isCurfew(): hour >= 21 || hour < 10. Window is [21:00, 10:00) spanning midnight.

🛡 Kill-resistant watchdog

A WorkManager periodic worker (15-min floor) persists across app-death and reboots. If an aggressive OEM power manager kills the service and cancels the alarms, the worker still fires: it locks directly (no service needed), then re-starts the service and re-arms the alarms.

6 · Putting it on your real Android TV

1
On the TV: Settings → Device Preferences → About, click Build 7× to unlock Developer options, then enable USB/Network debugging.
2
From your Mac, on the same network:
adb connect <TV-IP-address>:5555
adb install -r ~/Projects/tv-curfew/app/build/outputs/apk/debug/app-debug.apk
# grant the lock permission (works from adb shell, no root needed):
adb shell dpm set-active-admin com.tatav.tvcurfew/.CurfewAdminReceiver
adb shell am start-foreground-service com.tatav.tvcurfew/.CurfewService
3
Alternatively, open the TV Curfew app from the launcher and press “1. Enable Device Admin”, then “2. Start service”. Done — it now runs headless forever.

To remove it later

adb shell dpm remove-active-admin com.tatav.tvcurfew/.CurfewAdminReceiver
adb uninstall com.tatav.tvcurfew

7 · Honest limitations

  • Standby, not a true power-off. The panel backlight goes off and the system sleeps, exactly like the power button. A genuine cord-level shutdown is impossible without root — if your TV is rooted, swap lockNow() for Runtime.getRuntime().exec("su -c reboot -p").
  • Uses the device clock. If someone changes the TV's time, the window shifts with it.
  • OEM battery/power managers on some TVs may kill background services. This is handled by the WorkManager watchdog (boot receiver + foreground service + START_STICKY + a persisted 15-minute periodic worker that re-locks and re-arms everything). Worst-case re-lock latency if the whole app is killed is therefore ~15 min; instant otherwise. If a vendor is extremely aggressive, also whitelist the app from battery optimization.
  • Change the hours in one place: START_HOUR / END_HOUR in CurfewLogic.kt, then rebuild.
